William Shakespeare was born in April 1564 in the town of Stratford-upon-Avon, on England’s Avon River. When he was eighteen, he married Anne Hathaway. The couple had three children—an older daughter Susanna and twins, Judith and Hamnet. Hamnet, Shakespeare’s only son, died in childhood. The bulk of Shakespeare’s working life was spent in the theater world of London, where he established himself professionally by the early 1590s. He enjoyed success not only as a playwright and poet, but also as an actor and shareholder in an acting company. Although some think that sometime between 1610 and 1613 Shakespeare retired from the theater and returned home to Stratford, where he died in 1616, others believe that he may have continued to work in London until close to his death.

Merchent of Venice

The Merchant of Venice is a 16th-century play written by William Shakespeare. Set in Venice, the play follows the story of Antonio, a merchant, and his friend Bassanio, who is seeking to win the hand of Portia, a wealthy heiress. Antonio is unable to secure the funds himself, so he turns to Shylock, a Jewish moneylender, who agrees to grant him a loan on the condition that if Antonio fails to repay it, he will be forced to forfeit a pound of his flesh. Despite the danger of his predicament, Antonio pleads with Shylock to have mercy, and Portia is able to save Antonio by inferring a loophole in Shylock's agreement. The play is notable for its exploration of themes of justice, mercy, and prejudice.

Top Books

Hamlet is a tragedy written by William Shakespeare sometime between 1599 and 1601. It follows the story of Prince Hamlet of Denmark, who seeks revenge against his uncle Claudius for murdering his father, the King, and then succeeding him as the King of Denmark. Throughout the play, Hamlet struggles with his own morals and doubts as he is drawn into a dangerous revenge plot, ultimately leading to his own death. It is one of Shakespeare's most popular and widely performed plays.

Romeo and Juliet is a tragic play written by William Shakespeare. It tells the story of two young star-crossed lovers from the rival families of the Capulets and the Montagues. Despite their families' long-standing feud, Romeo and Juliet are deeply in love and secretly marry. After a series of misadventures, the two eventually take their lives in an attempt to be together in death. Romeo and Juliet is considered a timeless classic, with its themes of young love, self-sacrifice, and the power of fate still relevant today.

The Complete Works of William Shakespeare is a collection of all of the plays, poems, and other writings by the English playwright William Shakespeare. It was originally published in 1623 and is often referred to as the First Folio. The collection includes 38 plays, 154 sonnets, two long narrative poems, and several other poems. The plays include comedies, tragedies, and histories. The collection is an important part of the English literary canon and has been widely studied and performed throughout the world.
